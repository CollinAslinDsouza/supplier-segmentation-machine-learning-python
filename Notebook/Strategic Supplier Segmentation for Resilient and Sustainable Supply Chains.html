On-time delivery (mean 85%) and order accuracy (93%) are strong, though lead times vary widely (3–59 days), suggesting room for consistency improvement.

⚙️ Quality & Efficiency:¶

Defect rates average ~5%, with capacity utilization around 76%—healthy but with potential to optimize further.

🌍 Sustainability:¶

Carbon emissions and energy efficiency show wide variation, highlighting opportunities for greener practices. ESG certification is below 50%, suggesting limited adoption.

👥 Social & Governance:¶

Labor practices, community engagement, and transparency scores cluster around mid-levels (~5–6/10), pointing to moderate but improvable ESG maturity.

Need for Scaling:¶

The extreme difference in scale confirms the absolute necessity of the subsequent Standard Scaling step. If unscaled, features like Water_Usage would dominate the Euclidean distance calculation in the K-Means algorithm, effectively rendering the lower-magnitude scores (like Defect_Rate or any of the scores) irrelevant in forming clusters.

# The Elbow Method: Calculate Inertia for k=1 to k=9

inertia = []
K = range(1, 10) # Test k from 1 to 9

for k in K:
    # Initialize and fit the model for each k
    kmeans_model = KMeans(n_clusters=k, random_state=123, n_init=25)
    kmeans_model.fit(scaled_features)
    # Append the inertia (WSS) to the list
    inertia.append(kmeans_model.inertia_)

# Plot the Elbow Curve
plt.figure(figsize=(8, 6))
plt.plot(K, inertia, 'bx-')
plt.xlabel('Number of Clusters (k)')
plt.ylabel('Inertia (Within-Cluster Sum of Squares)')
plt.title('Elbow Method For Optimal k')
plt.grid(True)
plt.show()

import numpy as np
# Total Sum of Squares (TSS)
tss = np.sum(np.var(scaled_features, axis=0) * len(scaled_features))

# Within-Cluster Sum of Squares (WSS) = Inertia
wss = kmeans.inertia_

# Between-Cluster Sum of Squares (BSS)
bss = tss - wss

# Explained Variance (%)
explained_variance = (bss / tss) * 100
print(f"Explained Variance: {explained_variance:.2f}%")

### Above 70% is considered good 

Explained Variance: 13.40%

📊 Clustering Quality Interpretation¶

  • TSS (Total Sum of Squares): Captures the overall variation in the dataset.
  • BSS (Between-Cluster Sum of Squares): Reflects the variation explained by the clustering, i.e., separation between cluster centers.
  • Explained Variance (BSS/TSS): At 13.40%, the clusters explain only a modest portion of the total variation.

🔎 Insights¶

  • A low explained variance indicates that the clusters are not sharply separated, but it does not invalidate the model.
  • Clustering often reveals practical groupings even when statistical separation is weak—especially in complex, multi-dimensional ESG and KPI data.
  • The clusters can still provide actionable insights if interpreted carefully, e.g., identifying supplier segments with distinct trade-offs (speed vs sustainability, accuracy vs emissions).
  • Instead of discarding the model, the results should be used as a strategic lens: guiding partnerships, investments, and risk mitigation while acknowledging overlap between groups.

✨ In short: 13.40% variance is low, but the clusters remain valuable as directional insights rather than rigid boundaries.

🌍Analysis¶

Clustering suppliers using both operational KPIs and ESG metrics is a modern analytical approach that directly addresses today’s business challenges. In a globalized economy, companies face increasing pressure to balance efficiency, resilience, and sustainability. This type of modelling is valuable because:

- Risk Management:¶

It identifies high-risk suppliers (e.g., poor delivery but strong ESG) versus reliable partners, enabling proactive mitigation.

- Strategic Segmentation:¶

Firms can classify suppliers into tiers (e.g., agile, balanced, sustainable niche) and tailor engagement strategies accordingly.

- Sustainability Integration:¶

ESG clustering ensures that environmental and social responsibility are embedded into procurement decisions, not treated as afterthoughts.

- Operational Excellence:¶

By grouping suppliers with similar performance profiles, companies can streamline negotiations, benchmarking, and improvement programs.

- Resilience & Adaptability:¶

Clusters highlight trade-offs (speed vs. sustainability, cost vs. quality), helping businesses design flexible supply chain strategies that withstand disruptions.

📈 Strategic Use in Supply Chain Deployment¶

- Tiered Partnerships:¶

Prioritize “balanced” clusters for core operations, while leveraging “sustainable niche” suppliers for ESG-focused projects.

- Targeted Investments:¶

Direct capability-building programs to underdeveloped clusters, raising overall supply chain maturity.

- Policy Alignment:¶

Align procurement with corporate sustainability goals and regulatory requirements.

- Competitive Advantage:¶

Companies that integrate ESG into supplier strategy gain reputational benefits, investor confidence, and long-term resilience.

👉 In short: This modelling transforms raw supplier data into actionable strategy, enabling businesses to build supply chains that are efficient, sustainable, and future-ready.¶
